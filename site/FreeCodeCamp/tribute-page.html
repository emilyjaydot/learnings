Lee Krasner

"Mrs. Jackson Pollock"

Timeline of Lee Krasner's Life

1908 - Born in Brooklyn, New York to Ukrainian-Jewish immigrant parents.

1920s - Attended Washington Irving High School for Girls as they offered an art major. Attended the Women's Art School of Cooper Union on a scholarship after high school graduation. She also attended and graduated from the National Academy of Design.

1937 - Began taking classes from Hans Hofmann, who emphasized the two-dimensional nature of the picture plane and usage of color to create spatial illusion that was not representative of reality.

1940 - Joined the American Abstract Artists where she met artists Willem de Kooning, Arshile Gorky, Mark Rothko, and Barnett Newman.

1942 - First time seeing the work of Jackson Pollock, which caused her to reject Hofmann's cubist style of painting, which her own style was based on. Began a relationship with Jackson Pollock.

1945 - Marries Jackson Pollock in a church with two witnesses on the outskirts of East Hampton where the two shared a home. They each had a studio for creating art at their home.

1946 - Begins her Little Image series. Total of around 40 images that were the first that were from her imagination rather than a model. She would work on the series until 1949.

1950 - Krasner begins making color field paintings, gallerist Betty Parsons showed them in her gallery in a solo exhibition in the fall of that year.

1951 - After the solo exhibition, Krasner starts making collages on her color field paintings. This marks when she stopped working on an easel.

1953 - Her collage work was now incorporating Pollock's unwanted/discarded canvases. Some critics believe she was creating a sense of intimacy between themselves, which was lacking in their actual relationship.

1956 - Jackson Pollock dies in a single-car crash while driving under the influence of alcohol. his mistress was a passenger in the car, but survived the crash. Krasner was in Europe visiting friends with the crash occured.

1956 - Paints her Earth Green Series which critics consider to reflect her feelings of anger, guilt, pain, and loss she experienced about their relationship before and after he died.

1959 - Began suffering from insomnia. Painted at night under artificial light and painted in an aggressive style using white, grays, black, and browns. These are interpreted as violent and turbulent landscapes.

1965 - Had her first retrospective exhibition of her paintings at the Whitechapel Gallery in London. This was when art critics began to look at her and her work beyond just her relationship with Jackson Pollock.

1970 - Started collage paintings again, with drawings that she had drawn before 1940, that she had found while cleaning out her studio.

1977 - Solo exhibition of the collage paintings held at Pace Gallery in New York City. The show was very well received by critics.

1984 - Lee Krasner passes away at the age of 75 at New York Hospital.

1985 - The Museum of Modern Art (MoMA) in New York City held a retrospective exhibition of her work. She is only one of 4 women artists to have retrospectives at MoMA.

"(She) is a major, independent artist of the pioneer Abstract Expressionist generation, whose stirring work ranks high among that produced here in the last half-century."

-- The New York Times on her retrospective.
